Michael I. Jordan

Born February 25, 1956

"AI is not about mimicking human intelligence; it is about creating systems that augment human capabilities."

History

Michael I. Jordan, born in the United States, is a leading researcher in machine learning and artificial intelligence.
He started his career when he discovered the field of cognitive science during a visit to the University of California.
After Jordan earned a Ph.D. in cognitive science, he spent time at MIT before joining University of California in 1998.
As a professor in University of California, he obtained the Pehong Chen Distinguished Professorship.
Through his research, he helped bridge computer science with cognitive science, helping lead to the development of more sophisticated AI systems.
Because of his work in machine learning and cognitive science, he has received numerous honors, including the David E. Rumelhart Prize and the John von Neumann Medal.
